01~/about

I read human systems the way I was trained to read physical ones.

Physics taught me systems; software engineering taught me how products are actually built; years of language tutoring taught me how differently people think; copywriting taught me to be clear under commercial pressure. Delivery leadership is where all four converged — because most delivery failures live in the gap between what engineering means and what the business hears.

Systems thinker

I read an organization like a physical system — looking past the narrative for the hidden constraint. Teams rarely behave irrationally; systems often do.

Human translator

Engineers speak risk; product speaks outcomes; executives speak a third language. The highest-leverage act is usually interpretation — before any solution.

Three layers at once

I move fluently between executive strategy, flow analytics and tooling, and technical implementation — layers most leaders treat as separate.

06~/visionnext decade

Engineering my way

to what matters.

I don't see leadership as a title — I see it as a system to be designed. The next decade runs along three horizons.

Thesis

AI adoption isn't a technology transformation. It's a workflow, decision-making, governance, and operating-model transformation — the same class of problem I've been solving for years.

The hard part was never the tooling. It's organizational adoption.

Horizon 01·0–18 mo

Lead the next transformation

Stand up AI-enabled ways of working across an engineering organization and evolve its operating model around them — AI as an organizational capability, not a technology initiative.

H1
Horizon 02·18–48 mo

Help scaling and enterprise organizations turn strategy into execution through periods of transition: delivery modernization, flow optimization, operating-model design.
